Services

Clearcut

A method of regenerating a forest where all or most mature timber is harvested from a stand with a management plan in place to regenerate. This creates an even-aged stand with natural or artificially regenerated trees to the landowners specifications. Fun fact, cleared land creates peak wildlife environments in as little as one year after a cut or thinning.

Thinning

Most softwood stands need to be thinned twice through the course of their lifecycle to optimize average tree size (dbh).

Thinnings are designed to improve the health and value of existing timber stands. In fact, thinning is perhaps the single, most important silvicultural tool that one can employ to significantly improve one's forest. Choosing the right kind of thinning for your woodland depends on a number of complex factors, including site characteristic, stand age, species composition, stand acreage, and landowner objective. Thinnings greatly increase the growth rates of residual trees and allow for abundant vegetative growth, increasing the supply of food and cover for all types of wildlife. By removing the small, diseased, and defective trees you allow more sunlight, moisture and nutrients to reach your healthy trees; giving them room to grow, eliminating stress, and greatly reducing susceptibility to attacks by Southern Pine Bark Beetles. In addition to all of the listed benefits, thinning is an income producing venture for the landowner!

Thinning and harvesting create conditions that stimulate the growth of food sources for wildlife.

Management Plans

A plan created by a licensed forester for landowners to their exact specifications to optimize monetary value or sustain wildlife. We specialize in pine and hardwood management and will create a plan based on your specific needs. Examples of management plan goals: sustain wildlife, optimize wildlife compounds, manage hunting lands, or optimize tree value. If there are trees on your stand you want preserved, we will ensure they remain untouched through the process. We are here to help you reach your personal management goals, whatever they may be.

Openings created by harvesting provide habitat for deer and a variety of songbirds.

Salvage Harvests (storm damage)

If your wood has been damaged by a storm or hurricane, it still has value. Give us a call and we can give you a quote and alleviate the damage the storm did to your land.

  • Thinning

    forest management practice to remove some trees from a stand to give others more room and resources to grow

    forest management practice where most standing trees in area are logged at the same time with a clear plan in place for regeneration

  • Management Plan

    A plan written by a licensed forester that outlines your family's vision for your forest, describes the current forest condition, and outlines a plan of action to achieve your management goals

  • Appraisal

    An estimated value placed on your timber

  • Softwood

    the wood from a conifer (pine, fir, spruce)

  • Hardwood

    the wood from a conifer (pine, fir, spruce)

  • DBH

    diameter at breast height, how thick around a tree is

  • Forester

    a trained professional with expertise in planting, managing, and caring for trees

  • Timber Buyer

    a company representative who buys timber deeds and oversees that timber is cut and landowner is paid

  • Log Job
