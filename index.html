Clearcuts

A method of regenerating a forest where all or most mature timber is harvested from a stand with a management plan in place to regenerate. This creates an even-aged stand with natural or artificially regenerated trees to the landowners specifications.

1st Thinning

Thinnings, in addition to being an income producing venture for the landowner, are designed to improve the health and value of existing timber stands. Without thinnings, defective trees and genetically inferior trees will die off naturally. The 1st thinning allows the landowner to capitalize on this natural thinning process and accelerate remaining tree growth and quality. 1st thinnings usually take place when the stand reaches 12-15 years in maturity.

2nd Thinning

The 2nd, and likely last thinning a property needs before a harvest occurs is 5-7 years after the 1st thinning. This final thin allows only the highest grade trees to remain in your stand, creating peak stand conditions and ensuring a profitable harvest.
