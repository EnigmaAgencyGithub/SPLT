WE BUY TIMBER

Whether you're looking to thin, clearcut or manage your timber for wildlife, we're here to help. We have unrivaled industry experience and work hard to provide you with the best price possible for your timber.

We value your timberland. Let us help you prepare it for generations to come.

Why is it a good idea to cut your timber?

If you delay cutting your timber, it will begin to thin itself naturally. This loses you money and post pones the amount of time before you can cut again.

When to clearcut?

Once you have fully mature timber or a very dense stand it is in your lands best interest to harvest your timber. Our experienced staff can come cruise your stand and give you the best recommendation possible.

Vocabulary

  • Thinning

    forest management practice to remove some trees from a stand to give others more room and resources to grow

  • Clearcut

    forest management practice where most standing trees in area are logged at the same time with a clear plan in place for regeneration

  • Management Plan

    A plan written by a licensed forester that outlines your family's vision for your forest, describes the current forest condition, and outlines a plan of action to achieve your management goals

  • Appraisal

    An estimated value placed on your timber

  • Softwood

    the wood from a conifer (pine, fir, spruce)

  • Hardwood

    the wood from a conifer (pine, fir, spruce)

  • DBH

    diameter at breast height, how thick around a tree is

  • Forester

    a trained professional with expertise in planting, managing, and caring for trees

  • Timber Buyer

    a company representative who buys timber deeds and oversees that timber is cut and landowner is paid

  • Log Job

    the process of cutting, processing and transporting mature trees from a tract of land (the job site) to the mill

  • Saw Mill

    facility where logs are cut into timber

  • Tract

    a defined area of land

  • Swamp Harvest

    timber in standing water that “swamp loggers” are able to harvest

  • Salvage Harvest

    storm damaged timber that is harvested swiftly after damage, for profit
